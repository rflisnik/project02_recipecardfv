glazedDONUTS

  • 3 hr 30 min
  • 14 donuts
  • ingredients
    For the Donuts
  • 3 tablespoons active dry yeast or 2 1/4 teaspoons instant yeast
  • 1 cup whole milk, heated to 110˚F (43°C)
  • 2 to 2 1/2 cups bread flour, plus a bit for the work surface
  • 2 tablespoons superfine sugar (or just blitz granulated sugar in a blender until finely ground)
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon vanilla extract
  • 3 large egg yolks
  • 4 tablespoons unsalted butter (2 oz), at room temperature
  • Vegetable oil, for frying
    For the Glaze
  • 1 1/2 cups confectioners' sugar, sifted to remove any lumps
  • 3 to 4 tablespoons milk or water
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract (optional)

Glazed donuts. Everybody loves 'em. Even when seemingly everyone is paleo this and gluten-free that, you rarely see someone refuse a glazed donut. Especially when they're homemade, hot from the oil, and taste just like the old-fashioned kind you remember from childhood. The donuts are less sweet than most, which means you can drown them in glaze and they still won't seem cloying. Talk about our kinda donut.

    Donuts
  1. Dissolve 2 tablespoons yeast in 3/4 cup warm milk. Stir in 3/4 cup of flour to create a smooth paste. Cover and let rest in warm spot for 30 minutes.
  2. Combine the remaining warm milk and yeast. Add flour mixture along with sugar, salt, vanilla, and egg yolks. Mix until smooth. Add 1/2 cup of remaining flour. Mix on low for 30 seconds. Add butter and mix until it becomes incorporated, about 30 seconds. Add more flour, 1/4 cup at a time. Knead until dough pulls completely away from sides of bowl and not too sticky. Cover bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 1 hour.
  3. Line baking sheet with lightly floured dish towel. Roll out dough on lightly floured surface to 1/2 inch thick. With donut or cookie cutter, cut out 3-inch-diameter rounds with 1-inch-diameter holes. You can re-roll scraps and cut out additional holes.
  4. Place donuts at least 1 inch apart on baking sheet and cover loosely with plastic wrap. Let rest in warm spot to proof until they almost double in size, 5 to 20 minutes, peeking every five minutes. To test whether the dough is ready, touch it lightly with a fingertip.
  5. While donuts are proofing, heat heavy-bottomed pot with 2 inches of oil until thermometer registers 360˚F (182°C). With metal spatula, carefully place couple of doughnuts in the oil. Fry for 1 to 2 minutes per side, until light golden brown. Remove and drain on wire rack over paper towel, and let cool slightly before glazing. Repeat with the remaining donuts and holes.
    Glaze
  1. Place sugar in bowl and slowly stir in milk and vanilla a little at a time, to make a smooth, pourable glaze. Pour the glaze into shallow bowl. Dunk donuts, let any excess glaze drip off, and then transfer to wire rack placed on baking sheet or over sheet of parchment paper to rest until glaze sets. But really, who are we kidding? These glazed donuts are going straight from bowl to gaping mouth.
